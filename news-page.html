Textile Art Exhibition Launches in Abbeyleix

Hope Abaya, a recent graduate from the National College of Art and Design, brings her passion for tapestry to Abbeyleix Library this coming June.

Hope's work is inspired by her journey through the North Africa and Europe as she migrated from her home country in Sudan. Her work depicts the human condition in its relationship to nature and demonstrated how the beauty in nature can bring joy even on the most harrowing of life's journeys.

Hope's exhibition, 'What I saw on the road', combines her interest in working with tapestries into with her love of bright natural colours inspired by nature. Tapestry is a rare artform and allows for the creation of large rich depictions that delight the eyes and senses. Don't miss this unique and wonderful exhibitions by the richly talented Hope Abaya!

Art In Motion LIVE Demo

"A well-executed portrait is expected to show the inner essence of the subject (from the artist's point of view) or a flattering representation, not just a literal likeness", exclaims local artist Anna O'Meara. Anna fell in love with painting from a young age. Shorlty after her mother tragically died, she began painting her mother's portrait over and over again. "I was so afraid of losing her image, I decided to paint her in the likenesses I could remember".

Anna's practice of portrait painting developed into a passion for capturing 'the spirit within without' and she has continued to love painting people. Recently, Anna's work displayed at the National Gallery as part of an exhibition celebrating Irish people's struggle with self through our colonial past.

While live painting a local guest, Anna will guide attendees through a tutorial on how to capture the essence of people in paint. Anna is a brilliant lively personality who will delve excitedly into the history of portrait painting while she works. Not to missed. Bookings can made through Mountmellick Library.

Colouring The Future

Laois Arts Office are launching a new art competition to inspire kids of all ages to be more creative in imagining the County's future. 'Colouring The Future' invites local artists to create a picture of Laois at any time in the future. Children and teens are encouraged to send a message to our leaders by showing them what they want to see in their future."This is a wonderful opportunity to encourage creativity amongst our youth and to engage them in a meaningful with contributing to the County's development", announced the Mayor on launching the event in the Heritage Hotel.

Perfect Your Selfie

Local photographer Brian Mulcahy will lead a workshop for teens on taking photos for social media. The workshop will teach teens all about the different features on mobile cameras and will help them to understand how to capture high quality images. Students will learn about different lenses, focus techniques, angles, lighting and filters. Brian will also look at a variety of hardware like physical lenses that help students capture the perfect shot for their social media galleries.

But that's not all! Once the perfect picture has been taken, students will then be guided through basic photo editing. They will learn how to crop photos, edit colour pixels, resize images, add filters, text and overlays, as well as colur adjustments. Brian hopes to use the workshops to engage students in the art of of photographer and to inspire the young to become more creative in the process.

Bookings can be made through the Dunmase Theatre's main office and Brian is happy to cater to school groups as well.

Laois Photographic Society

Mountmellick Library launches a new Photographic Society this summer. The group will be formed by a number of local photographic enthusiasts with the aim of sharing their images of the county and its people through a community group.

The group aims to share photographic images of the county through the ages and will run workshops to teach photo taking and photo editing skills. The first workshop will focus on how to digitally enhance old photographs using modern photo editing technology.

The first meeting will take place in Mountmellic Library and the society will meet once monthly thereafter. Expressions of interest can be made through Mountmellick Library.
